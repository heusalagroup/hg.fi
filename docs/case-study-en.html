Case Study: How Job Seekers Experienced ChatGPT-Assisted Job Search

Research on using ChatGPT and AI in job search in practice

‚Üê Back to book website

I was curious to see how effectively AI could help in job search in practice. I conducted an experiment on LinkedIn where I offered job seekers the opportunity to find jobs using my book "AI in Job Search" and its methods. I asked participants for feedback on four questions:

  1. Did you find interesting job positions?
  2. Did you end up applying to any of them?
  3. Do you see value in using ChatGPT or my e-book's guidance for job search?
  4. Did you get a job or job offers?

Results and Experiences in Practice

Interesting Job Positions:

Almost all participants felt they had found interesting job positions, although there was variation in accuracy and relevance. Some felt that the search results were spot-on and saved time, while others faced challenges particularly with seniority and specific requirements.

Benefits Leading to Applications:

Some participants reported planning to apply to recommended positions. Participant #7 in particular clearly stated they had applied to suggested roles and strongly believed in the method.

Views on AI's Usefulness:

The majority of participants saw clear value in using ChatGPT and AI tools in job search. For example, participant #4 highlighted time savings and the opportunity to find jobs that might otherwise go unnoticed with traditional search methods. Participant #7 was strongly enthusiastic about it, saying AI usage was "ABSOLUTELY" beneficial.

Employment Still Open:

Since the experiment was recent, no concrete results were reported regarding employment, but several participants were hopeful and promised to share later if they get a job or offers.

Conclusions and Recommendations for Job Seekers

  • ChatGPT and AI clearly save time and bring out job opportunities that might otherwise go unnoticed.
  • Precise definition of your own skills and wishes significantly improves the accuracy of results.
  • The method is not perfect: in very specific tasks, user's critical evaluation and fine-tuning are still required.

This experiment confirms that the methods in the "AI in Job Search" book are practically useful and can significantly streamline job search ‚Äì as long as the user knows how to adapt the methods to suit their own situation.
